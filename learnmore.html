The Gap : Women and Non-Binary Individuals in Technology

───────────────────────────────



────────────

Through out their education, women non-binary individuals are systematically being guided away from technology, which restricts their access to, preparedness for, and opportunity to work in these professions and areas as adults.


Only 28% of technology related jobs are held by women and non-binary individuals, and men outweigh them in most technology undergraduate majors. In several of the future professions that will develop the fastest and provide the best wages, such as computer science and engineering, there are disproportionately large gender inequalities.

────────────





────────────

Key Factors Perpetuating Gender Technology Gaps

────────────

Gender Stereotypes

In addition to teachers and parents frequently underestimating girls' arithmetic skills beginning in preschool, technology related occupations in STEM are frequently perceived as being more masculine.

Fewer Role Models

Due to the dearth of female role models in books, media, and popular culture, girls and non binary individuals are less likely to be inspired to pursue careers in science and engineering. Even fewer Black women serve as role models in the sciences and math.

Male-Dominated Cultures

Because fewer women pursue STEM degrees and careers, these professions frequently maintain rigid, exclusive, male-dominated cultures that are not welcoming to or appealing to women and minorities.
